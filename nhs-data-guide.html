NHS Air Quality Data Integration Guide

How to populate the three-panel air quality widget (Long-term · Recent · Forecast) from the exposure service and London Air forecast. Written for NHS implementers integrating with Cerner or similar EHR systems.

All concepts · Design 3 workspace · Design 3.2 workspace

Exposure service API

Call the exposure service once per patient home location. A single request from three days ago through today provides everything needed for the Long-term and Recent panels.

Example request

Patient Eleanor Marsh, postcode sector SE1, hourly data from −3 days to now:

GET /api/v1/exposure?postcode=SE1&from=-3d&to=now&resolution=hourly
Accept: application/json
Authorization: Bearer <token>

What this response provides:
  • annual + annualYear — Long-term panel (year label, e.g. 2022)
  • recentDays[].hourly — compute daily DAQI banding for −3d, −2d, −1d, and today

Calculating DAQI banding (Recent panel)

UK Daily Air Quality Index (DAQI) uses pollutant-specific thresholds. Thresholds and colours match the daqi-vs-caqi reference implementation (colorScales.ts).

Step 1 — Daily mean per pollutant

For each day and each pollutant, average the hourly values returned by the exposure service:

dailyMean = sum(hourlyValues) / count(validHourlyValues)

Ignore null or missing hours. For “today”, use only hours received so far.

Step 2 — DAQI index per pollutant

Compare each daily mean against the lower-bound thresholds for that pollutant. The index is the highest band whose threshold the value meets or exceeds (levels 1–10):

PollutantThresholds (µg/m³) for levels 1–10
PM₂.₅0, 12, 24, 36, 42, 48, 54, 59, 65, 71
PM₁₀0, 17, 34, 51, 59, 67, 76, 85, 93, 102
NO₂0, 68, 135, 201, 268, 335, 401, 468, 535, 601
O₃0, 51, 101, 121, 141, 161, 181, 201, 221, 241

Step 3 — Take the highest index across pollutants

For each day, the displayed DAQI level is the maximum index across PM₂.₅, PM₁₀, NO₂, and O₃. This follows the UK approach of reporting the pollutant driving the overall band.

dayLevel = max(daqiLevel(pm25), daqiLevel(pm10), daqiLevel(no2), daqiLevel(o3))

Step 4 — Render the ladder

Display a 10-segment vertical ladder per day. Fill rule: segments 1 through dayLevel are coloured; segments above dayLevel are grey. Show the DAQI key strip below the Recent panel (Low · Moderate · High · V.High).

Forecast data source

Forecast banding comes from the Environmental Research Group (ERG) London Air forecast — not the exposure service.

  • URL: londonair.org.uk/data/londonair/LondonAirForecast.asp
  • Use banding level only — do not use a numeric index from the forecast page
  • Credit: display ERG logo below the Forecast panel (see Design 3.2a/3.2b)

Band → display index mapping

Map the text band to the highest index in that band, then fill all segments below:

Forecast bandDisplay indexSegments filled
Low31, 2, 3
Moderate61–6
High91–9
V.High101–10

Apply the same fill rule as Recent: colour segments 1..index, grey above.

Long-term bar mathematics (Designs 3.1 / 3.2)

The long-term panel compares annual mean concentrations to WHO air quality guidelines. It uses a separate scale from DAQI — do not mix annual WHO comparison with daily index levels.

WHO guidelines (µg/m³)

PollutantWHO annual guideline
PM₂.₅5
PM₁₀15
NO₂10
O₃60

Bar height

The WHO dotted line is fixed at 52% of the bar track height.

Below the line (concentration up to the WHO guideline):

if annual ≥ WHO: belowHeight = 52% of track else: belowHeight = (annual / WHO) × 52%

Above the line (excess over WHO):

excessRatio = (annual − WHO) / WHO aboveHeight = (excessRatio / maxExcessAcrossPollutants) × 48% of track

The pollutant with the largest excess ratio fills the zone above the line; others scale proportionally.

Display conventions

  • Show annual concentrations as integers (µg/m³)
  • Show ratio as 3.6× — no “WHO” suffix on the multiplier
  • Label the year from annualYear (e.g. 2022)
  • Use pollutant bar colours (solid above WHO, light fill below); names in the key strip below the panel

Panel-by-panel summary

Long-term

  • Data: annual object + annualYear from exposure service
  • Render: four vertical bars (PM₂.₅, PM₁₀, NO₂, O₃), shared WHO dotted line at 52%, ratio above bar, concentration below
  • Key strip: pollutant colour swatches + names below the panel

Recent

  • Data: recentDays[].hourly from exposure service (−3d through today)
  • Process: daily mean per pollutant → DAQI index per pollutant → max across pollutants
  • Render: four day columns (−3d, −2d, −1d, Today) with 10-segment DAQI ladders
  • Key strip: DAQI band legend below the panel

Forecast

  • Data: text band from London Air forecast
  • Process: map band to display index (Low→3, Moderate→6, High→9, V.High→10)
  • Render: single DAQI ladder for tomorrow; band name and date below
  • Credit: ERG logo below the panel

Widget header

Use the title “Air Quality at patient's home” followed by patient name and location sector (e.g. · Eleanor Marsh · SE1).
